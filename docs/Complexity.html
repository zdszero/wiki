Measuring

big-O

Say that \(f(n) = O(g(n))\) if postive integer \(c\) and \(n_o\) exist such for every integer \(n \ge n_o\),

\[f(n) \le cg(n)\]

small-O

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be functions: \(N \rightarrow R^{+}\), if

\[\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{f(n)}{g(n)}\]
